Ankle Sprain

An ankle sprain occurs when the ligaments that support the ankle are overstretched or torn. In basketball, this commonly happens during landing after a jump, sudden changes in direction, or stepping on another player's foot.

Common Symptoms

  • Swelling around the ankle joint
  • Pain when walking or putting weight on the foot
  • Bruising or tenderness
  • Feeling of instability

Recovery Process

Recovery depends on the severity of the sprain. Returning to sport too early increases the risk of re-injury.

  • Days 1–3: Rest, apply ice, and avoid pressure on the ankle.
  • Days 4–7: Begin gentle movement, stretching, and balance exercises.
  • Return to Play: Only when pain-free, full movement is restored, and strength has returned.

R.I.C.E. Protocol

  • Rest: Stop playing and protect the ankle.
  • Ice: Apply for 15–20 minutes every few hours.
  • Compression: Use an elastic bandage to control swelling.
  • Elevation: Raise the ankle above heart level when resting.

Injury Prevention

  • Balance training on one leg
  • Ankle strengthening using resistance bands
  • Learning proper landing mechanics
  • Wearing supportive footwear
Personal Reflection: After experiencing an ankle sprain myself, I realised how often players rush back into games without understanding proper recovery. This section was designed to prevent that mistake.

Knee Pain

Knee pain in basketball is often caused by repetitive jumping, poor landing technique, muscle imbalances, or overuse without adequate recovery.

Prevention Focus

  • Strengthening quadriceps and hamstrings
  • Improving jump and landing form
  • Proper warm-up routines

Finger Injuries

Finger injuries usually occur from contact with the ball, rebounds, or collisions with other players.

Common Issues

  • Jammed fingers
  • Sprains
  • Dislocations

Hamstring Strain

Hamstring strains occur during sprinting, sudden acceleration, or rapid changes in speed.

Key Prevention Strategies

  • Dynamic warm-ups
  • Strengthening posterior chain muscles
  • Avoiding sudden workload increases
